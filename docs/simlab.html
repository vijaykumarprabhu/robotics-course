Lab Course in Simulation¶

This year we’ll have to do the lab course in simulation! The goal still is to learn about robot manipulation, integrating methods for motion generation and perception, potentially also machine learning, task planning, and mobile navigation.

The major time is spend on practically solving (coding) a series of problems, with direct supervision by the instructors during the session. In some lectures the instructor introduces basic concepts. The series of exercises includes, for instance,

  • generation of basic motion on the robot system,
  • leveraging motion planning and optimization,
  • perceiving objects and mapping them into virtual representations,
  • integrating perception and motion generation for grasping, pushing and placing objects

Students are expected to work also offline, as homework, on these problems. After going though these problems the teams spend the rest of the course working on a final challenge. This year the challenge might be to build high constructions from randomly dropped objects.
